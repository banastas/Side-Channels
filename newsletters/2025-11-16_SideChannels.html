The Camera Protects Us From What We're Looking At

blog.banast.as

Susan Sontag wrote that the camera creates psychological distance - a shield between you and what's happening in front of you. The person holding the camera is never really in the moment they're capturing. They're framing it, adjusting exposure, thinking about composition. Last year I went through every photo from Flynn's first five years and realized I remember the photos better than I remember the moments. The image became the memory. The memory of being there - really being there - faded. When you pick up a camera you're choosing documentation over participation, choosing to organize experience rather than be dissolved by it. Sometimes that's the right choice. Sometimes it's not. The trick is knowing the difference.
